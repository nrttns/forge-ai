## 1. Purpose

This document defines the permanent Forge AI mission and product-development model for AI-DOS as a reusable AI Operating System. It explains why Forge AI exists, what Forge AI owns, what AI-DOS is, how AI-DOS is developed without merging product and Target identities, how AI-DOS becomes distributable, how independent Target Projects consume AI-DOS without receiving the private development repository, how evidence from authorized Targets may contribute to AI-DOS evolution, how Human Governance controls capability evolution and release maturity, what the AI Highway means, and how Forge AI planning documents must remain aligned.

This document is strategic and normative for Forge AI mission alignment. It does not implement AI-DOS, create an RFC, define a transport schema, update live state, replace planning authorities, certify readiness, or authorize release.

---

## 2. Forge AI Identity

Forge AI is:

```text
The AI-DOS Product Development, Distribution,
Validation, Evolution, and Autonomy Enablement Target Project.
```

Forge AI is responsible for developing, validating, packaging, distributing, operating, evolving, and governing AI-DOS as a reusable AI Operating System.

Forge AI is not AI-DOS. Forge AI is the first self-hosting and dogfooding Target Project for AI-DOS, but it is not the only source of AI-DOS product evidence. Forge AI owns its Target Project mission, planning, live operational state, authorization, evidence requirements, protected areas, and acceptance constraints. Forge AI also owns product-development planning for AI-DOS subject to Human Governance.

---

## 3. AI-DOS Identity

AI-DOS is:

```text
A reusable AI Operating System that provides governed,
bounded, evidence-driven operating capabilities to
independent Target Projects through distributable runtimes
and stable public interfaces.
```

AI-DOS provides reusable operating behavior for planning, bounded execution, validation, review, reporting, blocker handling, evidence production, and governed capability evolution. AI-DOS must remain Target-independent and reusable across authorized Target Projects.

AI-DOS is not:

- the Forge AI Target Project;
- a documentation bundle copied into every Target repository;
- a universal ProjectStatus;
- a universal DevelopmentPhases model;
- a universal Target roadmap;
- unrestricted autonomous execution;
- a Target-specific implementation framework;
- an AI host such as Codex, ChatGPT, Claude, or an IDE agent; or
- an automatic product-release authority.

AI-DOS product truth belongs to the AI-DOS product space. It must not absorb Target Project truth, Target live state, Target roadmaps, Target protected-area rules, or Target-specific validation commands.

## 4. Target Project Identity

A Target Project owns:

- its mission;
- planning;
- live operational state;
- source code;
- architecture;
- protected areas;
- validation commands;
- execution authorization;
- evidence and acceptance requirements.

A Target Project does not own AI-DOS internal product truth. A Target Project consumes AI-DOS through governed runtime distributions and stable public interfaces, not by receiving or depending on the private Forge AI development repository.

## 5. Mission Statement

Forge AI exists to develop, validate, distribute, operate, and evolve AI-DOS as a reusable AI Operating System through bounded execution, independent Target evidence, governed product evolution, progressive autonomy, and Human-Governed release maturity.

---

## 6. Mission Pillars

1. **Develop AI-DOS as an AI Operating System.** Forge AI advances reusable operating capabilities rather than Target-specific scripts or isolated documentation.
2. **Preserve product purity and Target independence.** AI-DOS must remain reusable and must not absorb Forge AI, Axis Suite, customer, enterprise, open-source, or other Target-specific truth.
3. **Maintain the private development / public distribution boundary.** Private AI-DOS development may occur in Forge AI product-development space, but users, AI hosts, and independent Targets should consume governed runtime distributions.
4. **Produce versioned, governed AI-DOS distributions.** Distribution must become a first-class product lifecycle concern with evidence and Human Governance gates.
5. **Establish stable public system interfaces.** AI-DOS must expose stable public interfaces before supported adapters and providers depend on it.
6. **Support independent Target operation.** AI-DOS must operate against Target-owned context, authority, validation, and evidence requirements without requiring private product-repository access.
7. **Validate through self-hosting and external Target evidence.** Forge AI dogfooding is necessary but not sufficient; independent Target evidence is required for external readiness and AI Highway maturity.
8. **Capture reusable capability gaps from any authorized Target.** Any authorized Target may reveal AI-DOS improvement opportunities when evidence, privacy, reuse, and governance checks are satisfied.
9. **Govern AI-DOS product evolution.** Product changes require authorized product-development planning and Human Governance; AI-DOS may not self-authorize changes.
10. **Increase autonomy progressively and safely.** Autonomy advances through evidence-backed capability levels, not by unrestricted access or silent scope expansion.
11. **Preserve privacy, security, evidence, and traceability.** Product learning must be minimized, provenance-aware, integrity-verifiable, privacy-reviewed, and consent-controlled where external transfer is involved.
12. **Build and operate the Multi-Target AI Highway.** Forge AI develops the product and operating ecosystem through which AI-DOS safely serves independent Targets and learns from privacy-safe evidence.
13. **Keep Human Governance final.** Human Governance controls capability acceptance, release decisions, autonomy claims, and product maturity declarations.
14. **Prevent automatic acceptance.** Tests, merges, evidence generation, implementation completion, or review findings do not automatically become product acceptance, capability acceptance, or release approval.

## 7. Product–Project Separation

AI-DOS product truth must not absorb Target Project truth.

Target Projects must not require access to the private AI-DOS development repository.

| Concern | Owner |
| :--- | :--- |
| AI-DOS reusable product truth | AI-DOS product space |
| AI-DOS private development source | Forge AI product-development environment |
| AI-DOS distribution artifacts | Governed release and distribution system |
| AI-DOS public system interface | AI-DOS |
| Forge AI mission and planning | Forge AI Target Project |
| Forge AI live operational state | Forge AI ProjectStatus |
| Independent Target truth | Each Target Project |
| Product evolution decisions | Forge AI planning plus Human Governance |
| Product release acceptance | Human Governance |
| Target execution acceptance | Target-owned Human Governance or authorized authority |

Forge AI may authorize bounded product-development work because AI-DOS is the product Forge AI develops. That authority does not merge Forge AI Target identity with AI-DOS product identity, does not allow AI-DOS to own Forge AI ProjectStatus, and does not allow a Target Project to become the source of AI-DOS internal truth.

## 8. AI-DOS Product Lifecycle

The normative AI-DOS product lifecycle is:

```text
Private AI-DOS Development Repository
    ↓
Architecture, Product Development, and Validation
    ↓
Build and Packaging
    ↓
Signed / Versioned AI-DOS Distribution
    ↓
Local CLI / Local MCP / Managed Provider
    ↓
Independent Target Project
    ↓
Execution, Validation, Review, and Evidence
    ↓
Privacy-Safe Product Evolution Input
    ↓
Governed AI-DOS Product Improvement
    ↓
New Distribution Release
```

This is a product lifecycle, not an automatic runtime loop. Each transition requires the applicable evidence and Human Governance gate. Execution evidence can inform product evolution only after privacy, provenance, reuse, integrity, and authorization checks. Product improvement does not automatically create a release; release requires a distinct governance decision.

---

## 9. Distribution Model

AI-DOS distribution maturity advances through progressive stages:

```text
Distribution v1 — Local CLI Package
Distribution v2 — Local MCP Server
Distribution v3 — Managed / Hosted AI-DOS Provider
```

These stages are progressive maturity stages, not three unrelated products. Local CLI precedes Local MCP. Stable public interfaces precede supported adapters. Local operation should preserve Target privacy by default. Hosted operation requires stronger security, isolation, retention, consent, audit, and operational controls.

Distribution planning does not imply implementation, readiness, certification, or release. Users and AI hosts should consume a governed AI-DOS runtime distribution, not the private Forge AI development repository.

---

## 10. AI Host and Provider Model

Codex, ChatGPT, Claude, IDE agents, and other supported AI hosts are not AI-DOS. They may invoke AI-DOS, but AI-DOS remains the governed runtime responsible for bounded operating behavior.

```text
User
    ↓
AI Host
    ↓
AI-DOS Public System Interface
    ↓
AI-DOS Runtime Distribution
    ↓
Authorized Target Project
```

AI hosts invoke AI-DOS through public interfaces or supported adapters. AI hosts must not depend on AI-DOS internal documentation paths. Target repositories must not contain AI-DOS private architecture and development state. AI-DOS runtime remains responsible for governed operating behavior, while the Target remains responsible for Target truth, authority, validation, protected areas, evidence, and acceptance.

## 11. Governed Self-Application

Forge AI self-application is dogfooding and one evidence source within the larger product-development model.

```text
Forge AI supplies a governed AI-DOS product objective
    ↓
AI-DOS operates against Forge AI as a Target Project
    ↓
AI-DOS performs bounded work
    ↓
Execution and validation evidence are produced
    ↓
Forge AI and Human Governance evaluate the result
    ↓
Accepted evidence may contribute to product evolution
```

Self-application is not the sole evidence source, is not external Target proof, does not authorize self-approval, and must not collapse Forge AI and AI-DOS identities. AI-DOS must also learn from independent Target evidence before external readiness and AI Highway maturity can be claimed.

Product-development evidence may originate from any authorized Target Project, including:

```text
Forge AI
Axis Suite
Independent customer Target
Enterprise Target
Open-source Target
Future supported Target types
```

```text
Opportunity Source:
Any authorized Target Project.

Product Evolution Owner:
Forge AI product-development planning and Human Governance.
```

An opportunity discovered in a Target does not authorize modification of that Target or AI-DOS. It becomes eligible product-development input only after evidence, privacy, reuse, and governance checks. Target execution acceptance remains with the Target-owned Human Governance or authorized authority; product evolution acceptance remains with Forge AI planning and Human Governance.

## 13. Capability Evolution

The governed AI-DOS product-evolution lifecycle is:

```text
Target Execution Evidence
    ↓
Potential AI-DOS Capability Gap
    ↓
Opportunity Capture
    ↓
Opportunity Validation
    ↓
Privacy and Reuse Review
    ↓
Human Governance Authorization
    ↓
Bounded AI-DOS Product Change
    ↓
Regression, Purity, and Target-Independence Validation
    ↓
Human Governance Capability Acceptance
    ↓
Distribution Release Decision
```

AI-DOS may detect and report opportunities. AI-DOS may not automatically add opportunities to active product work, self-authorize product changes, promote capabilities, or release distributions. Passing tests does not equal capability acceptance. Capability acceptance does not automatically equal release. Release requires its own governance gate.

## 14. Evolution Input Portability

AI-DOS shall never assume future access to the originating Target Repository.

Eligible product-evolution input must be portable and privacy-safe so that product learning can survive beyond the originating Target without extracting unnecessary Target truth. This document does not define implementation schemas, protocols, file formats, or APIs.

Eligible evolution input must be:

- portable;
- evidence-backed;
- minimized;
- privacy-reviewed;
- provenance-aware;
- integrity-verifiable;
- consent-controlled where external transfer is involved;
- reusable beyond the originating Target.

## 15. AI Highway

The AI Highway is:

```text
A governed product and operating ecosystem through which
AI-DOS distributions safely serve independent Target Projects,
collect privacy-safe capability evidence, and produce improved
future releases without exposing private development truth or
crossing Target authority boundaries.
```

Conceptually, the AI Highway operates as:

```text
Private Product Development
    ↓
Governed Distribution
    ↓
Independent Target Operation
    ↓
Evidence and Capability Learning
    ↓
Governed Product Evolution
    ↓
Improved Distribution
    ↓
More Independent Targets
```

AI Highway does not mean unrestricted autonomy, uncontrolled telemetry, mandatory hosted operation, Target data extraction, cross-Target leakage, bypassed Target authority, or automatic release. It is a governed product and operating model for safe multi-Target AI-DOS distribution, learning, improvement, and maturity.

## 16. Autonomy Definition

Autonomy is the ability of AI-DOS to complete increasingly complex, multi-step, bounded work with less direct intervention while preserving explicit Target authority, product and Target separation, validation, traceability, escalation, privacy, safe-stop behavior, and Human Governance.

Autonomy must support product distribution and multi-Target operation without becoming a claim of unrestricted control.

Autonomy must not mean:

- unrestricted repository access;
- self-approval;
- automatic release;
- automatic capability promotion;
- silent scope expansion;
- mandatory telemetry;
- cross-Target leakage;
- bypassing Target authority;
- bypassing Human Governance.

---

## 17. Autonomy Capability Ladder

Autonomy matures through progressive capability levels. Each level requires evidence and Human Governance acceptance. No level is claimed as achieved merely because this document exists.

| Level | Name | Capability | Required Governance Boundary |
| :---: | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 0 | Human-Directed Execution | Executes explicit instructions with close human direction. | Human-scoped instruction only. |
| 1 | Context-Aware Assistance | Resolves supplied Target Context and provides grounded assistance. | Target Context must be explicit. |
| 2 | Bounded Planning | Produces scoped plans, risks, inputs, and validation strategy. | Planning approval required before execution. |
| 3 | Bounded Execution | Performs approved work within explicit scope and file boundaries. | Protected areas and Target authority enforced. |
| 4 | Self-Validation | Runs required checks and reports defects or validation gaps. | Validation evidence required; validation is not approval. |
| 5 | Review and Escalation | Reviews outputs, reports uncertainty, and escalates blockers. | Review findings go to authorized authority. |
| 6 | Recovery and Replanning | Recovers from bounded failures without expanding scope. | Recovery remains inside original authorization. |
| 7 | Autonomous Workflow Completion | Completes approved workflows from intake through evidence report. | Completion does not imply acceptance. |
| 8 | Governed Continuous Improvement | Identifies evidence-derived improvement opportunities. | Proposals only; no self-authorization. |
| 9 | Multi-Target Operation | Operates across isolated independent Target Contexts. | Separate Target authority per Target. |
| 10 | Governed Product Evolution | Converts authorized product-learning evidence into bounded AI-DOS product changes. | Human Governance authorizes and accepts capability changes. |
| 11 | AI Highway Operational Maturity | Supports repeated governed distribution, independent Target operation, privacy-safe learning, and improved releases. | Human Governance controls maturity and release claims. |

## 18. Safety Invariants

- Human Governance remains final.
- Target Context is explicit.
- Product and Target identities remain separate.
- Private development truth is not exposed by default.
- Target truth remains Target-owned.
- Scope is bounded.
- Protected areas are enforced.
- No authority is inferred.
- No lifecycle transition is automatic.
- No capability promotion is automatic.
- No release is automatic.
- Validation is mandatory.
- Review is distinct from approval.
- Capability acceptance is distinct from release.
- Blockers cause safe stop or escalation.
- Results remain traceable.
- Target Contexts remain isolated.
- Evidence transfer is privacy- and consent-bound.
- AI-DOS must remain reusable and Target-independent.

| Invariant | Enforcement Intent | Violation Response |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Human Governance remains final | Prevent self-approval, automatic acceptance, and authority inversion. | Stop and escalate for human decision. |
| Product and Target identities remain separate | Prevent AI-DOS product truth from absorbing Target truth. | Halt contamination and require governed correction. |
| Private development truth is protected | Prevent private repository exposure as a distribution mechanism. | Refuse exposure and redirect to governed distribution. |
| Target Contexts remain isolated | Prevent knowledge leakage and authority crossover. | Stop multi-context work and isolate evidence. |
| No lifecycle transition is automatic | Prevent unauthorized phase, capability, release, or state drift. | Require explicit Human Governance gate. |
| Validation and review are not approval | Preserve evidence without converting it into acceptance. | Submit evidence to the proper authority. |
| Evidence transfer is privacy- and consent-bound | Prevent uncontrolled telemetry or Target data extraction. | Minimize, redact, or block transfer pending authorization. |

## 19. Evidence Model

AI-DOS product and autonomy claims require evidence sufficient for Human Governance to evaluate authorization, behavior, validation, risks, and maturity. Evidence categories include:

- product-development evidence;
- distribution evidence;
- installation and compatibility evidence;
- execution evidence;
- validation and review evidence;
- opportunity evidence;
- privacy and redaction evidence;
- capability-evolution evidence;
- release evidence;
- external Target evidence;
- repeated multi-Target evidence.

Evidence must identify what was authorized, what was done, what was not done, which Target Context was used, which distribution or product capability was exercised, what validation occurred, what review found, what blockers appeared, what data was minimized or redacted, what governance gate applied, and whether the responsible authority accepted the result.

Forge AI succeeds when AI-DOS can repeatedly:

```text
be built and distributed;
be invoked by supported AI hosts;
operate against independent Target Projects;
preserve Target authority and isolation;
perform bounded work;
validate and review;
safe-stop on blockers;
produce evidence;
capture reusable product-learning input;
improve through Human-Governed evolution;
release new compatible distributions.
```

Document production alone is not product proof. Self-hosting alone is not external readiness. A single external pilot is not Multi-Target AI Highway maturity. Passing tests alone is not capability acceptance, and capability acceptance alone is not release approval.

## 22. Decision Rules

Every proposed Forge AI task should be challenged with:

1. Does this directly advance reusable AI-DOS product capability?
2. Does this preserve product purity?
3. Does this strengthen private/public boundaries?
4. Does this improve distribution, integration, operation, validation, evolution, or release maturity?
5. Does this produce observable reusable behavior?
6. Is it supported by evidence?
7. Is it bounded and independently validatable?
8. Does it preserve Target isolation and privacy?
9. Does it avoid speculative architecture and repository-support work?
10. Is the current ProjectStatus state authorized for this work?
11. Does Human Governance retain the final acceptance or release decision?

If the task fails these tests, it must be rejected, deferred, or escalated.
