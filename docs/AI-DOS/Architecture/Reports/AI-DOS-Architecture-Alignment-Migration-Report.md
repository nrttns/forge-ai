### Directory Ownership and Placement Rules
- `Architecture/Constitution/` owns constitutional authority only.
- `Architecture/RFC/` owns normative architecture RFC families; Boundary, Runtime, Engine Platform, Engine Specializations, and Distribution are separate families.
- `Architecture/Discovery/` owns historical discovery, audit appendices, and non-normative evidence.
- `Architecture/Reports/` owns architecture reports and migration records; reports do not become normative architecture.
- `Architecture/Standards/` owns standards and standards schemas.
- `Meta/`, `System/`, `Operational/`, `Commands/`, `Workflows/`, and `Templates/` retain their established product responsibilities.
- Validation, testing, certification, lifecycle, checklist, and tooling directories remain product support surfaces, not Target planning truth.
### RFC Placement and Numbering Rules
- A.1 remains constitutional and is not reused for RFC numbering.
- A.2 belongs to the Boundary RFC family.
- A.3 belongs to Runtime.
- A.4 and A.4.x belong to Engine Platform.
- A.5.0 through A.5.12 belong to Engine Specializations.
- A.6 belongs to Distribution.
- No RFC family README owns repository-wide RFC authority.
### Report, Evidence, and Navigation Rules
- Discovery artifacts are explicitly historical and non-authoritative.
- Architecture reports record evidence and migration decisions only.
- `docs/AI-DOS/README.md` owns top-level product navigation; `Architecture/README.md` owns architecture navigation; `Architecture/RFC/README.md` owns RFC-family navigation; family READMEs own only their family.
