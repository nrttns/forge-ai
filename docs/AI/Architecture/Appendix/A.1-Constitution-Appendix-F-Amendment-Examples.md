# Appendix F — Constitutional Amendment Examples

> Parent: A.1 — Constitution
> Version: 3.0.0-beta

## Purpose

This appendix provides canonical examples demonstrating how constitutional
amendments are proposed, evaluated, approved, rejected, and recorded.

## Amendment Lifecycle

Proposal
↓
Evidence Collection
↓
Governance Review
↓
Human Approval
↓
Implementation
↓
Publication
↓
Historical Archive

---

## Example AME-001 — Minor Amendment

Scope:
Terminology clarification.

Evidence:
Terminology conflict report.

Decision:
Approved.

Impact:
No constitutional invariant changes.

## Example AME-002 — Major Amendment

Scope:
New governance principle.

Evidence:
Architecture audit, governance review.

Decision:
Approved by Human Governance.

Impact:
Framework Core documents updated.

---

## Example AME-003 — Emergency Amendment

Trigger:
Critical constitutional defect.

Requirements:

- Human approval
- Immediate publication
- Mandatory retrospective review

---

## Example AME-004 — Rejected Amendment

Reason:

- Insufficient evidence
- Conflicts with constitutional invariants

Disposition:
Rejected and archived.

---

## Example AME-005 — Deferred Amendment

Reason:

Requires additional evidence.

Status:
Deferred.

---

## Amendment Metadata

Every amendment shall include:

- Amendment ID
- Author
- Date
- Evidence IDs
- Reviewers
- Human Approval
- Version
- Affected Sections

---

## Validation Rules

- Human approval mandatory
- Evidence mandatory
- Traceability mandatory
- Version history preserved
