# Purpose

This template defines the standard structure for every Stage document.

A Stage represents one architectural milestone within a Phase.

A Stage owns planning.

It does not own implementation.

---

# Required Structure

## 1. Status

Document status, parent Phase, Stage identifier, historical Sprint mapping, and planning hierarchy.

## 2. Stage

Stage identifier, official name, parent Phase, and milestone.

## 3. Parent Phase

Relationship to parent Phase.

## 4. Historical Sprint Mapping

Sprint identifier(s) and migration notes.

## 5. Current Status

Planned, active, completed, or historical.

## 6. Purpose

Why this Stage exists.

## 7. Architectural Goal

Measurable milestone achieved by this Stage.

## 8. Scope

Included and excluded work.

## 9. Responsibilities

What this Stage owns.

## 10. Boundaries

What this Stage must not own.

## 11. Dependencies

Previous Stages, future consuming Stages, assumptions.

## 12. Inputs

Phase architecture, previous outputs, RFCs, audits.

## 13. Outputs

Contracts, specifications, decisions, validation requirements.

## 14. Deliverables

Documentation, implementation, validation, architecture updates.

## 15. Allowed Work

Approved work categories.

## 16. Forbidden Work

Prohibited activities.

## 17. Validation Requirements

Architecture review, dependency validation, ownership verification, tests.

## 18. Stage Completion Criteria

Conditions required for completion.

## 19. Stage Completion Checklist

Checklist.

## 20. Risks

Known limitations, deferred work, open questions.

## 21. Related Sprints

Historical Sprints belonging to this Stage.

## 22. Related Audits

Relevant audits.

## 23. Related RFCs

Relevant RFCs.

## 24. Related Documents

References.

## 25. Next Stage

Next architectural Stage.
